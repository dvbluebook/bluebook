DVHS "A-G" Course List

When completing the application for admission for the University of California and State University systems, you may enter the courses listed below. Be sure to enter them exactly as they appear on your transcript.

A: Social Science/History: World Geography, World History I/II, AP European History, AP World History, U.S. History, AP U.S. History, American Government, Government Cohorts: Social Justice, AP Government and Politics: Comparative, AP Government and Politics: United States

B: English: English 9, English 10, English 11, AP English Literature, AP English Language and Composition, British Literature, English 12, Expository Reading and Writing, Novel, Shakespeare, Science Fiction and Fantasy Literature

C: Math: Algebra 1, Algebra 1 Enriched 1, Algebra 1 Enriched 2 (Completion of Algebra 1 Enriched 1 and Algebra 1 Enriched 2 fulfills the Algebra 1 requirement), Algebra 2, Accelerated Algebra 2, Advanced Math Topics, Calculus, AP Calculus A/B, AP Calculus B/C, Geometry, Pre- Calculus with Trigonometry, Honors Pre-Calculus with Trigonometry, Statistics, AP Statistics

D: Laboratory Science: Biology, AP Biology, Marine Biology, Chemistry, Honors Chemistry, AP Chemistry, Physics, Honors Physics, Anatomy and Physiology, Honors Anatomy and Physiology, Biological Sciences Laboratory Research ROP, Biotechnology Accelerated and Research ROP, Environmental Chemistry, AP Environmental Science, Integrated Physical Science (meets the “D” requirement for CSU only), AP Physics C: Mechanics, Computer Science C++ (VS Net)**, AP Computer Science A**

E: Language other than English: Chinese Mandarin 1, 2, 3, Honors Chinese Mandarin 4, AP Chinese Language and Culture, Chinese Mandarin II for Heritage Learners, Chinese Mandarin III for Heritage Learners, French 1, 2, 3, Honors French 4, AP French Language and Culture, Spanish 1, 2, 3, Honors Spanish 4, AP Spanish Language, Korean 1, 2, 3, Korean 4 Honors, Korean 5 Honors, Spanish 1, 2, 3, Spanish 4 Honors, AP Spanish Language and Culture. College courses in a language other than English may be used, including American Sign Language, Japanese, Cantonese, etc.

F: Visual and Performing Arts: 3D Art 1, 3D Art 2, Art 1, Art 2, Advanced Art 5, AP Art History, Computer Graphic Arts, Computer Graphic Arts 2, Digital Photography, AP Studio Art: Drawing, Art of Video Production, Digital Arts Design for the Web, Dance 1, 2, 3 and 4, AP Music Theory, Concert Band, Jazz Band, Symphonic Band, Symphonic Band 2*, Symphonic Wind Ensemble*, Wind Ensemble, Orchestra, Chamber Orchestra, Chamber Singers, Concert Choir, Treble Choir, Men’s Ensemble, Women’s Ensemble, Oral Interpretation, Theatre Arts 1, Theatre Arts 2, Theatre Arts 3, Theatre Production

G: College Preparatory Electives: All advanced courses listed above plus: AP Computer Science**, VS. Net**, AP Research, AP Seminar, Academic Leadership, Culinary Arts*, Principles of Engineering, Sports Medicine, Advanced Sports Medicine, AP Seminar, AVID Senior Seminar, Journalism 1, Journalism 2*, Journalism 3, Publications, Advanced Publications, Advanced Speech and Debate, Mind Matters*. AP Macroeconomics*, AP Microeconomics, Philosophy*, Intro to Residential and Commercial Contruction*

*New courses pending UC/CSU “A-G” approval for 2019-2020 school year

** Computer courses meet the “D” requirements for UC only

Grades used in Calculating Admissions Eligibility: Grades earned in the 10th and 11th grades in the courses listed above are used to calculate eligibility. Courses taken in the summer following the 9th grade and taken in the summer following the 11th grade will also be used. If you earned any D’s or F’s in any of your required high school courses, you will report these grades for the UC’s and the CSU’s. You will have the ability to report the repeated course grade. You will need to compute your GPA with repeated courses.

Honors/Advanced Placement Courses Earning Weighted Grades: Only the following courses will be given a “weighted” grade: Any AP (Advanced Placement) course, Honors Anatomy and Physiology, Honors Chemistry, Honors Physics, Honors Pre-Calculus, Honors Chinese Mandarin 4, French 4 Honors, Korean 4 Honors, Korean 5 Honors, Spanish 4 Honors.

Note: Graduation Credit for Online, Off-Campus and College Courses: SRVUSD will provide credit towards graduation for completion of accredited online courses, off-campus courses and college courses. A total of 40 credits from non-district courses can be included on the official SRVUSD transcript, as long as the course(s) is/are completed after June 1, 2019.

Note from UC: "We limit the number of honors points to 4 courses (8 semesters), with no more than 2 courses (four semesters) from 10th grade."
